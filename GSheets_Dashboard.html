Root-Cause Analysis
Why Sep 23 became the worst day in the period
🔴
CAUSE 1: Launch Spike → Return Wave — Sep 17 recorded $97K gross (highest single day). Sep 18 added $44K. This bulk ordering created a predictable ~6-day return lag that hit Sep 23 as a concentrated wave.
🔴
CAUSE 2: High-Return SKUs Shipped En Masse — Scuba Pant (49.5% return rate) and Kirsten Mock Neck (37.9%) were almost certainly the primary campaign SKUs. Bulk orders of broken-fit items → bulk returns 6 days later.
🔴
CAUSE 3: Ad Spend Blind to Returns — $15,050 continued firing on Sep 23 while net revenue collapsed. ROAS appeared 3.58× on reported sales — completely hiding the −33.2% CM. ROAS is not profitability.
🟡
THE LESSON: Monitor returns in real-time alongside spend. Implement a 7-day post-launch pause rule: reduce ad spend after any day with gross sales >2× the daily average until the return window passes.

Key findings and action items

Google Best ROAS (3.39×) on only 23% of budget. Reallocating 10% from Meta ($39K) → Google could drive ~$132K incremental attributed revenue at existing ROAS. Priority: Shopping + branded keywords.
